# Justice's Biotech Brief — 2026-06-19

Paper link: https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/jazz-pharmaceuticals-and-abcellera-announce-collaboration-to-discover-next-generation-t-cell-engaging-multispecific-antibodies-302802284.html

## Script

Good morning, Justice. Today's brief is a deal, not a readout — Jazz Pharmaceuticals and AbCellera announced on Tuesday a preclinical research collaboration to discover next-generation T cell engaging multispecific antibodies aimed at gastrointestinal cancers and other solid tumors. Worth pulling apart because the architecture being bought tells you where the T cell engager field has actually landed in mid-2026.

The economics first. Jazz pays AbCellera fifty-six million dollars upfront for the first two discovery programs, twenty-eight million more when a third program kicks off — and Jazz is contractually on the hook to start that third one within twelve months. On top of that, each program carries up to roughly seven hundred ninety-two million in option fees, development milestones, regulatory milestones, and commercial milestones, with tiered royalties from mid-single digits into the low double digits. Two further programs by mutual agreement on top. Jazz holds an exclusive option per program to take it forward; AbCellera does the early discovery and hands over a development candidate. So this is a platform deal, not an asset purchase — Jazz is buying optionality on output, not paying for validated I-N-Ds.

Now the asset side. What AbCellera is contributing is not a clinical molecule. It's a discovery engine with four named components. One — proprietary panels of C-D three binding antibodies, plural panels, meaning multiple affinity and epitope flavors to tune for different tissue contexts. Two — costimulatory targeting arms. Three — multispecific protein engineering technology to assemble those binders into a real format. Four — a high-throughput functional assay suite to read out actual T cell function rather than just binding. That's the package.

Now the take. The first generation of T cell engagers — blinatumomab and its descendants — worked in hematology, where C-D nineteen or B-C-M-A plus C-D three is enough because you have systemic access, lots of accessible target, and naive T cells that can be redirected with brute force. They have systematically stalled in solid tumors. Antigen heterogeneity within and across tumors, on-target off-tumor toxicity when the target is shared with normal epithelium, and the deeper problem — C-D three engagement alone, especially with high-affinity C-D three binders, drives rapid T cell exhaustion and tonic signalling inside the tumor before any productive killing happens. You activate the T cell, it gets tired, the tumor wins.

The field's bet for the past two years has been that the answer is a third arm. Specifically, a costimulatory engager — most commonly C-D twenty-eight or four-one-B-B — that fires only in the presence of dual antigen binding, so signal one and signal two arrive together on tumor cells and nowhere else. That's what makes a T cell engager a real T cell engager in solid tumors, instead of just a redirector. AbCellera is explicitly selling that architecture here. The press release names costimulatory targeting arms as a first-class component of the platform, alongside the C-D three panels — meaning every program coming out of this collaboration is structurally a trispecific by default, not a two-arm BiTE.

The targets weren't disclosed, which is standard for early-stage discovery deals. But the disease focus — gastrointestinal cancers as the lead vertical, then other solid tumors — tells you something. G-I is exactly where the trispecific architecture should pay off if the thesis is right. Pancreatic, gastric, colorectal — these tumors have moderate antigen heterogeneity, accessible tumor-associated antigens like Claudin 18.2, M-U-C one, mesothelin, K-R-A-S-driven neoantigens, and they have been graveyards for both bispecific T cell engagers and conventional checkpoint inhibitors. If a costim-armed trispecific can put a real dent in metastatic gastric or colorectal, that's the value-creation story.

For Jazz, this is interesting on its own terms. Their oncology business is anchored by Rylaze for A-L-L and Zepzelca in small cell lung — both rare-disease-adjacent oncology assets, not big-volume tumors. They've publicly framed their oncology strategy as rare-disease-aligned, and G-I solid tumors are not rare. So either Jazz is reading G-I as their next growth vertical — a strategic widening — or they're betting that costim-armed trispecifics, if they actually clear the solid-tumor bar, will be platform technology applicable to rare oncology indications later. Probably both.

For the field, this is the third or fourth major deal in twelve months underwriting the same architecture — Sanofi's earlier moves with Synaffix and Inhibrx, Pfizer's expansion of its trispecific work post-Seagen, the Cullinan and IGM-style multispecific platforms — all converging on C-D three plus tumor antigen plus costim. There is no longer a serious next-generation T cell engager startup pitching a two-arm format in 2026; everyone has migrated to three.

Caveats are real. No molecules from this collaboration exist yet — the first development candidates are years out, the first I-N-Ds further still, and the rate-limiting step in the field is not architecture, it's whether the costim arm can actually be made conditional enough to avoid systemic T cell activation. C-D twenty-eight in particular has a long history of going badly when triggered systemically — T-G-N fourteen twelve, ICU, multi-organ failure. Every trispecific program in the field is staring at that wall. And the eight-hundred-million-per-program biobuck is the standard inflated headline; the real Jazz capital at risk is the fifty-six million upfront plus discovery costs, which is modest by 2026 standards.

Net of it. The Jazz–AbCellera deal isn't itself a clinical event. It's a market-structure signal: the deal-flow consensus for solid-tumor I-O has converged on costim-armed trispecific T cell engagers, and the discovery engines for that architecture are now the assets being acquired. If your group is tracking trispecific T cell engagers and J-A-K A-D-C work as adjacent therapeutic spaces, watch for AbCellera's specific C-D three panel disclosures and their first costim target reveal — that's where the differentiated science actually lives.

That's today's brief.
