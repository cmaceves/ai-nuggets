BioNTech and Bristol Myers Squibb head into ASCO twenty twenty-six with pumitamig — B-N-T three-two-seven, also known as B-M-S nine-eight-six-five-four-five — as the most-watched late-stage readout in their joint portfolio. Number two. Akari Therapeutics dropped a preclinical data package this past week supporting an ASCO twenty twenty-six abstract titled "Combination synergy of spliceosome modulator A-D-C with a K-RAS inhibitor in K-RAS-mutated pancreatic cancers." The asset is A-K-T-X one-oh-one — a TROP-two-targeted antibody-drug conjugate, but with a payload that is not topoisomerase-one. Instead, the warhead is a proprietary R-N-A spliceosome modulator called P-H-one, which degrades pre-messenger-R-N-A transcripts in tumor cells. In K-RAS-G-twelve-D and G-twelve-C-mutated pancreatic models, A-K-T-X one-oh-one combined with a K-RAS inhibitor produced synergistic anti-tumor effects, materially beyond the additive expectation. This matters for two reasons. First, every other clinical-stage TROP-two A-D-C — Trodelvy, Datroway, Merck and Kelun's sac-T-M-T — uses a topoisomerase-one warhead, which means resistance to the payload class is already a clinical concern. A non-topo-one payload opens a chemistry lane that does not exist anywhere else in the TROP-two field. Second, the synergy with K-RAS inhibition is the kind of combination biology that yesterday's RevMed spotlight flagged as the next phase of the RAS program — direct K-RAS inhibitors will need rational A-D-C partners to drive deep responses and forestall resistance. Akari is preclinical, and the asset is years from clinic, but the chemistry argument is the right kind of payload-platform thinking for Skaggs.

Number three. Legend Biotech announced its ASCO twenty twenty-six lineup last Thursday with a rapid oral session for L-B-twenty-one-oh-two — the company's D-L-L-three-targeted, dominant-negative T-G-F-beta-receptor-two-armored autologous C-A-R-T-cell therapy in relapsed or refractory small-cell lung cancer and large-cell neuroendocrine carcinoma. This is the preliminary Phase one safety, tolerability, and efficacy readout. The dose-escalation design is a modified three-plus-three across planned dose levels of zero-point-three, one, two, four, eight, twelve, and sixteen million C-A-R-positive T-cells per kilogram. The teaser language from Legend says no dose-limiting toxicity through two million cells per kilo, with a dose-dependent efficacy signal at higher doses and response correlating with C-A-R-T expansion. D-L-L-three is one of the cleanest tumor-restricted antigens in small-cell lung cancer biology — Amgen's tarlatamab B-iTE built a franchise on it. C-A-R-T against D-L-L-three is a step beyond — the durability profile in a relapsed-refractory S-C-L-C population, if it holds, is the kind of data that re-opens the cell-therapy-in-solid-tumors thesis that has frustrated the field for a decade. Calibr's in-vivo C-A-R-T strategy with AbbVie ultimately has to compete with autologous data points like this one.
