Good morning Travis, this is your Calibr-Skaggs daily briefing for Wednesday, May twentieth, twenty twenty-six. Here are today's pharma headlines.

Number one. Wave Life Sciences delivered the biggest platform readout of the day. Their drug WVE dash zero zero six, an A-D-A-R-recruiting GalNAc oligonucleotide for alpha-one antitrypsin deficiency, hit its mark across both biweekly and monthly dosing in the RestorAATion two trial. At four-hundred milligrams once a month, patients showed nearly sixty-eight percent reduction in mutant Z-A-A-T, plus wild-type M-A-A-T production at almost sixty percent of total A-A-T, reaching the protective M-Z-like phenotype. Wave is the spotlight today.

Number two. Relay Therapeutics announced initial clinical data for zovegalisib, a mutant-selective P-I-three K alpha inhibitor, in P-I-K-three-C-A-driven vascular anomalies. Sixty percent of patients hit volumetric response at twelve weeks, with grade three adverse events at only nine percent versus thirty to seventy-one percent for Novartis' alpelisib, brand name Vijoice. Nearly all patients reported symptom improvement. Wall Street is modeling close to three billion in peak sales for this indication alone, and the readout strengthens the case that mutant-selective P-I-three K alpha inhibition can become a chronic-use therapy where the pan-class drugs fail on tolerability.

Number three. Oorja Bio launched out of stealth with thirty million in Series A funding from Westlake Village BioPartners. The C-E-O is Sujay Kango, formerly chief commercial officer at Acceleron during the Reblozyl and sotatercept years. The lead asset, O-R-J dash zero zero one, is a peptide beta-one integrin agonist that targets alveolar epithelial type two cells, the progenitor population whose dysfunction is upstream of idiopathic pulmonary fibrosis. Phase one in sixty-four healthy volunteers showed no systemic toxicities, and the company has I-N-D clearance to start Phase two in I-P-F patients this year. This is a meaningfully different mechanism from the existing standard of care, which is Boehringer's nintedanib and Roche's pirfenidone.

Number four. Incyte signed a strategic deal with Edison Scientific to embed Edison's continuous-learning A-I scientist, Kosmos, across target discovery, validation, and translational biology. Financial terms were undisclosed. This is one more datapoint that mid-cap pharma is now buying the A-I scientist as a platform, not just licensing predictions from a vendor.

Number five. Parabilis Medicines filed its S-one yesterday, planning to list on Nasdaq under ticker P-B-L-S. This is the same Parabilis that announced its two-point-three billion-dollar Helicon-conjugate deal with Regeneron the day before, which we covered in yesterday's spotlight. The S-one tells you the Series F cash and the Regeneron upfront were not enough for them to push the lead anti-beta-catenin Helicon into late-stage trials without retail capital. Watch the pricing range when it shows up in the next amendment.

Number six. Pfizer's vaccine chief Annaliesa Anderson told Fierce Biotech that Pfizer is, quote, comfortable, end quote, stepping into the hantavirus outbreak on a cruise ship and the Bundibugyo Ebola epidemic now flagged by W-H-O as a public health emergency. Neither pathogen has a licensed vaccine. Pfizer has not committed to specific programs, but Anderson signaled the company would not sit out on its m-R-N-A platform if either becomes a sustained public health need.

Number seven, and we'll close on policy. The White House added more than six hundred generic medicines to the TrumpRx direct-to-consumer site through Mark Cuban's Cost Plus Drug Company, Amazon Pharmacy, and GoodRx. The administration says the platform has now saved patients more than four hundred million dollars since the February launch. Generics are about ninety percent of U-S prescription volume, so this is a meaningful expansion of the platform's footprint, but the brand-name negotiation track that branded biotech actually cares about remains stuck in the same Inflation Reduction Act framework the Supreme Court declined to disturb on Monday.
