Number two — Vertex's other story, which matters directly to Calibr's autoimmune thesis. Povetacicept — the dual BAFF-APRIL antagonist Vertex paid four-point-nine billion for when it bought Alpine Immune Sciences in 2024 — has now been BLA-filed for accelerated approval in IgA nephropathy. Vertex used a Priority Review Voucher to compress the FDA review to six months. The Phase 3 RAINIER interim analysis hit hard — 52 percent reduction in proteinuria from baseline, 79 percent reduction in galactose-deficient IgA1 versus placebo, 85 percent hematuria resolution. This is a B-cell-and-plasma-cell-tuning biologic that does not deplete — it modulates BAFF and APRIL signaling. So the autoimmune B-cell market is now a three-way fight: deplete with CAR-T, deplete with bispecifics like UCB's cizutamig, or modulate upstream like povetacicept. The bear case on Calibr's CD19 sCAR-T is that for IgAN-grade inflammation, modulation may be enough. The bull case is that lupus, lupus nephritis, and refractory myositis remain depletion-required. Watch the AURORA-LN lupus nephritis cohort of povetacicept's basket study for the next read.

Number three — Cytokinetics is reading out ACACIA-HCM topline today. Eight a.m. Eastern conference call. This matters because non-obstructive HCM has no approved therapy, the prior MAPLE-HCM head-to-head against Bristol's mavacamten was a clean win, and a positive ACACIA opens the second wedge of the aficamten franchise. Not directly Calibr-adjacent, but a major franchise-defining cardiology readout.

Number four — Celcuity reported Phase 3 VIKTORIA-1 results in the PIK3CA-mutant cohort over the weekend. The triplet of gedatolisib plus fulvestrant plus palbociclib delivered 9.3 months median PFS versus 2.0 months for fulvestrant alone, hazard ratio zero-point-two-four — a 76 percent reduction in progression risk. The doublet hit 7.4 months PFS, hazard ratio zero-point-three-three. Gedatolisib is a pan-PI3K-mTOR inhibitor — broader than alpelisib's PI3K-alpha selectivity — and the data position it as the new standard of care in HR-positive HER2-negative breast cancer. Detailed results at ASCO late-breaker May 29. Celcuity stock up 14 percent. The PDUFA for the wild-type cohort already filed is July 17, with the PIK3CA-mutant sNDA going in next.
