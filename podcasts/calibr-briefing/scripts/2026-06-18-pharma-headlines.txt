Good morning. It's Thursday, June eighteenth, twenty twenty-six, and this is your Calibr-Skaggs Daily Briefing. Yesterday was a dense day for major therapeutic milestones — the F-D-A signaled it will accept uniQure's three-year Phase one-two data as the primary basis for an accelerated-approval B-L-A for the first potential disease-modifying therapy in Huntington's disease, Jazz Pharmaceuticals and AbCellera signed a T-cell-engager collaboration potentially worth seven-hundred-ninety-two million dollars per program, the cardiac-genetic-disease biotech Kardigan priced an upsized four-hundred-million-dollar I-P-O this morning, F-2-G and Shionogi posted positive Phase three data for the first novel-mechanism antifungal in over twenty years, and the PACAP-migraine startup Vedana Therapeutics emerged from stealth with a forty-six-million-dollar Series A. Today's spotlight is the uniQure announcement and what it implies for rare-C-N-S gene therapy, accelerated-approval pathways for single-administration genetic medicines, and the broader question of how the F-D-A is now treating durable one-and-done interventions. Five items in the roundup.

First, uniQure and the F-D-A. Yesterday uniQure announced that following a Type B meeting, the F-D-A communicated that the three-year analysis from the Phase one-two study will be acceptable as the primary basis for a Biologics License Application seeking accelerated approval for A-M-T one-thirty in Huntington's disease, with the B-L-A filing planned for the third quarter of twenty twenty-six. A-M-T one-thirty is an A-A-V-five vector delivering a micro-R-N-A against the huntingtin gene, administered as a single procedure via M-R-I-guided convection-enhanced stereotactic delivery directly into the striatum — both caudate and putamen. The September twenty twenty-five three-year topline showed a seventy-five-percent slowing in composite U-H-D-R-S progression for the high-dose arm compared to a propensity-matched external control derived from the Enroll-H-D natural history dataset, p-equals point oh-oh-three, and a sixty-percent slowing in Total Functional Capacity, p-equals point oh-three-three. The F-D-A also asked the company to consider using a concurrent standard-of-care control rather than a sham procedure in the post-approval confirmatory trial. This is the largest single regulatory step yet for huntingtin lowering as a disease-modifying mechanism, after a decade of high-profile failures including Roche and Ionis's tominersen, Wave Life Sciences's allele-selective antisense efforts, and Novartis's branaplam splice modulator. Today's spotlight in a few hours unpacks the mechanism, the trial design, the competitive landscape, and the read-through to Calibr's switchable-C-A-R-T platform and rare-disease regulatory posture.

Second, Jazz Pharmaceuticals and AbCellera. Yesterday Jazz announced a preclinical research collaboration with AbCellera to discover next-generation T-cell-engaging multispecific antibodies for gastrointestinal cancers and other solid tumors. Financial terms — fifty-six million dollars upfront for the first two programs, an additional twenty-eight million if a third program is initiated within twelve months, and up to seven-hundred-ninety-two million dollars per program in development, regulatory, and commercial milestones, plus mid-single-digit to low-double-digit royalties. The structure is a Jazz option-and-license arrangement on AbCellera-discovered candidates — Jazz pays for the discovery work and exercises options as molecules advance. AbCellera's microfluidic single-cell antibody discovery platform sits in the same assay-layer category we mapped yesterday around Protillion's massively-parallel protein-display flow cells, and yesterday's three-layer A-I-antibody-design competitive map — model layer, assay layer, integrated pipeline — applies directly. AbCellera is now executing across all three layers, and the Jazz arrangement is a clean assay-platform engagement that lets Jazz buy optionality on a credible solid-tumor multispecific engine without committing to integrated-pipeline economics. The T-cell-engager modality is increasingly the dominant immune-oncology approach outside of checkpoint inhibition — Amgen's blincyto, Regeneron's Lynozyfic, and Johnson and Johnson's tecvayli set the early hematologic standards, and solid-tumor T-cell engagers are the unsolved problem the Jazz-AbCellera deal explicitly targets.

Third, Kardigan. This morning the cardiac-genetic-disease biotech Kardigan priced an upsized initial public offering of twenty-five million shares at sixteen dollars each, raising approximately four-hundred million dollars and beginning trading today on the Nasdaq under the ticker K-A-R-D. Kardigan is the fourth biotech this year to raise four-hundred million or more in I-P-O proceeds — a level of capital formation not seen in the biotech I-P-O market since twenty twenty-one. The company was founded by former MyoKardia executives — that's the same MyoKardia that developed Camzyos and was acquired by Bristol-Myers-Squibb for thirteen billion dollars in twenty twenty-one — and emerged from stealth in January twenty twenty-five with close to six-hundred million dollars in private venture funding. Lead asset is danicamtiv, a cardiac myosin activator originally discovered at MyoKardia, licensed back to Kardigan from Bristol in twenty twenty-four, and now positioned for genetically-driven dilated cardiomyopathy — a condition with no approved targeted therapies. Phase two-b-slash-three data are expected in the first half of twenty twenty-seven. The strategic message is that capital markets are again funding cardiac-genetic biotech at scale, a sector that had been largely starved of capital between the MyoKardia exit and the obesity and cardiometabolic wave of the last two years.

Fourth, F-2-G and Shionogi. This morning the two companies announced positive topline results from the global Phase three OASIS study evaluating oral olorofim versus AmBisome — liposomal amphotericin B — followed by standard of care in invasive aspergillosis. The trial met its primary endpoint of noninferiority on all-cause mortality at day forty-two — twenty-three-point-eight percent for olorofim versus twenty-four-point-three percent for AmBisome plus standard of care. The mechanism is what matters. Olorofim is a first-in-class inhibitor of dihydroorotate dehydrogenase in fungal pyrimidine biosynthesis, and if approved would be the first novel-mechanism antifungal for invasive aspergillosis in over twenty years. The previous standards of care — voriconazole and the other azoles, the echinocandins, amphotericin B — were all defined by the early two-thousands. F-2-G received a complete response letter on its first N-D-A filing in twenty twenty-three, raised one-hundred million dollars in twenty twenty-four to support resubmission, and the Phase three OASIS readout positions a resubmission in the U-S. Antifungal resistance — particularly azole resistance in aspergillus — has been steadily growing, and a novel-mechanism backbone addresses that head-on. F-2-G handles the U-S submission and Shionogi handles Europe and Asia under their twenty twenty-two strategic collaboration.

Fifth and last, Vedana Therapeutics. Yesterday the Seattle-based migraine-prevention biotech Vedana emerged from stealth with forty-six million dollars in Series A financing, co-led by Westlake BioPartners and Canaan Partners with participation from Dawn Biopharma and Alexandria Venture Investments. The lead program is a next-generation antibody targeting PACAP — pituitary adenylate cyclase-activating polypeptide — and the second program is a dual antibody targeting both PACAP and C-G-R-P. PACAP and C-G-R-P sit in adjacent migraine-pathway biology. The C-G-R-P antibody class — Aimovig, Ajovy, Emgality, Vyepti — has been the most successful targeted migraine prevention story in two decades, but a meaningful fraction of patients have insufficient response, and PACAP is the leading hypothesis for the next add-on mechanism. Lundbeck's anti-PACAP antibody lu-A-G zero-nine-two-two-two in Phase three is the most advanced competitor. Vedana's founding team includes leaders from the original C-G-R-P-antibody discovery wave, and the strategic bet is that combining PACAP plus C-G-R-P in a single subcutaneous self-administered antibody gives the next-generation migraine-prevention asset — the same dual-mechanism single-molecule playbook that Spyre is running in I-B-D and that Calibr is running in M-A-S-H.
